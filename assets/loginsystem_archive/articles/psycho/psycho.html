Case Study on Psychology based on Superheroes  
Introduction  
Images of superheroes have been used in art and literature for a long time.  
As a rule, the superhero is a protagonist and get the sympathy of readers and  
Viewers. Such heroes decide to break the law and take justice into their own hands  
after they have witnessed a crime or become a victim. In popular culture,  
superheroes have a range of specific qualities. They may vary but remain  
unchangeable in general terms:  
1. Double life. In order to protect their families and friends from the  
revenge of criminals or arrest, vigilantes lead double lives and hide  
their real identities.  
2. Moral code. Although a vigilante cannot follow the law in all the  
decisions, he or she sticks to the code of honor. For example, some  
heroes refuse to use a firearm or blackmailing.  
3. Abilities. As a rule, a superhero has special skills or superpowers in  
the first place or receives training that allows him or her to surpass the  
criminals physically or intellectually.  
The image of the superheroes has become extremely popular in the movies,  
series, and comic books about superheroes. However, vigilantes exist in our reality.  
They are not a figment of the imagination.  
For the case study, the character of Superman will be analyzed. Superman  
is a DC Comic book initially written by Jerry Siegel and Joe Shuster. The first  
comic book was the publisher in April 18,1938. In brief, he is an alien from  
another planet before his home planet was destroyed. He was adopted by farmer

Learning Psychology in old days  
One of the greatest conflicts in this comic book is he wear cape and no  
mask, literally revealing himself to the world. It can be considered that people of  
those days want someone to stand up against crimes and they want it publicly.  
People of those days wants perfect man, considering the facts that he possesses  
several abilities. The moral code of ‘no killing’ make himself vulnerable against  
enemies.  
Comparing to present days  
Nowadays, people want superheroes more secretly. Showing face in public  
is a dumbest thing to be done. Superheroes becomes more logical and they possess  
double life. They even got a team to support them secretly (The Flash). It can be  
concluded that people of today care more about team work and brotherhood than  
personal victory.  
In conclusion, People in general create heroes and enjoy having them. And  
heroes in general have double life, moral code and abilities. People of previous  
days want heroes in publicly and a perfect man whereas today’s people want hero  
to save secretly fused with family and team work.
